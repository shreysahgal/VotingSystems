About Approval Votes

The idea behind approval rating is that voters indicate which candidates they approve of and the candidate who is approved of most wins. Because the input file is a preference schedule, this is a Top 2 Approval Method because it assumes that the voters approve of their top 2 choices equally. Practically, this system can be thought of as a sort of borda count where the top 2 choices of each voter are given equal points. Like a Borda Count, Approval Votes also run the risk of violating the Majority Critereon; it is possible for a candidate with the most first-place votes to lose because first and second place votes are weighted equally.
