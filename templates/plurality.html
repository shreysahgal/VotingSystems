About the Plurality Method

The plurality method of voting simply counts how many first-place votes each candidate receives and the winner is the candidate with the most first-place votes. This voting method is very common in the United States. Ties are possible with this method and would have to be determined using a runoff vote. This system is sometimes confused with the majority voting system but the two are different: in a majority voting system, a candidate only wins if they recieve more than half of the total votes cast whereas in a plurality system, the winner doesn't necessarily have more than have of the votes, just more than the other candidates. When Note that when there are only 2 candidates on the ballot, the majority and the plurality are equal.
