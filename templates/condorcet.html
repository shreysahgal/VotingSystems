About the Condorcet Method

The Condorcet method does pairwise comparisons of the candidates and compares them head-to-head. If candidate A wins the matchup over candidate B, A gets 1 head-to-head point. Similarly, if B wins, B gets 1 head-to-head point. If it is a tie, A and B both get 0.5 points. Once each candidate is compared to each other candidate, the candidate with the most points wins. The winner using the Condorcet method is different than what is referred to as the "Condorcet Winner": a candidate is the Condorcet Winner if and only if they win every head-to-head match-up with each other candidate.
