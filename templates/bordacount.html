About the Borda Count

The Borda Count method gives candidates points based on what rank they were on each voters' preference ballot. The higher up a candidate is on the ballot, the more points they get. For example if there are 3 candidates running for president and Stacy gets 10 first-place votes, 3 second-place votes, and 5 third-place votes, she will, 10*3 + 3*2 + 5*1 = 41 Borda count points. Once the total points for each candidate is determined, the candidate with the most points wins. There is a potential problem with this method, though: it is possible for a candidate with the majority of first-place votes to lose because a different candidate was in general higher up on voters' ballots. This violates the Majority Critereon which states that if a candidate receives the majority of first-place votes, they should win the election.
